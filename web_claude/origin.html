— The Place —

Where goji has grown for a thousand years.

The micro-region around Helan Industry Park sits at 38° North — the latitude that has produced China's reference-grade goji for generations. Two hundred days of sunshine, alkaline mountain soil, and silt carried down from the Yellow River meet here.

Our 12,000-mu plantation has cultivated this combination for two decades. The same berries, processed through the same operation, become every form in our portfolio.

— The Practice —

Organic cultivation, full traceability.

Certified to EU BCS Organic, USDA NOP, and JAS Organic standards via Kiwa, our agriculture and handling are inspected annually. Each lot is traceable back to the field row it was harvested from.

Pesticide screening at 510-point coverage. BRCGS Grade B for food safety. CNAS-accredited in-house laboratory.

— The Harvest —

Hand-picked, sun-finished,
locked in fresh.

Red goji is harvested by hand at peak ripeness, then air- or sun-dried to lock in the polysaccharide and carotenoid profile. The "Fresh Lock" line is sealed within hours of picking for whole-fruit retail.

Black goji — a separate Lycium ruthenicum variety from the high-altitude plateau — is harvested for its anthocyanin and polyphenol content, used in premium beauty and antioxidant products.
